# Equity for Humanity — Founding Concept Brief v1.0

> **Status:** Equity for Humanity is a founding public concept, not yet a registered charity, foundation, trust, securities offering, or investment product. The current goal is to invite collaborators, test the model, and explore legal and governance options.

## Mission

**Equity for Humanity is a proposed permanent public-benefit endowment designed to help every human being share in a growing portion of global prosperity through universal ownership.**

Our community is humanity. Our benefit is universal.

Equity for Humanity — A founding public concept to help every human being share in this generational AI transformation, so all of humanity can benefit and flourish.

## Why now

AI may become one of the greatest engines of wealth creation in history. If ownership remains concentrated, its benefits will be concentrated too. Because AI is built from humanity’s accumulated intelligence, language, culture, science, code, and creativity, humanity has a moral claim on some share of the prosperity AI helps create.

AI, secure digital wallets, and transparent blockchain records can make a global ownership system far more efficient, auditable, and accessible than earlier generations could have imagined.

Those who benefited from past and present growth can help current and future generations share in this next wave of growth — and help younger generations adapt to this new AI paradigm.

This is not a replacement for work, purpose, creativity, public AI initiatives, or national AI strategies. It is a growing support system for global individual security, human agency, and flourishing.

## Core idea

Equity for Humanity is a proposed public-benefit endowment for universal shared ownership.

For efficiency, the model focuses on one global fund: broad ownership of productive global assets, long-term compounding, and dynamic growth-linked distributions that let growth continue working for humanity over time.

The goal is not to pick AI winners. It is to own productive global growth broadly, efficiently, and transparently, as AI permeates the global economy. The investment model can be roughly based on broad global indexing, such as the FTSE Global All Cap Index or similar globally diversified benchmarks.

## Founding principles

Ambitiously grand. Operationally simple.

The model should be designed for optimal administrative efficiency, broadest reach, and hyper-growth — while protecting dignity, privacy, and trust.

Core principles:

1. **Simple** — easy to understand, explain, audit, and participate in.
2. **Universal** — the long-term intended beneficiary is every human being.
3. **Hyper-efficient** — low fees, low overhead, automation where appropriate, and minimal bureaucracy.
4. **Equitable** — everyone can participate without proving poverty, worthiness, nationality, ideology, or productivity.
5. **Self-sustaining** — the fund reinvests enough to grow over generations.
6. **Self-recycling** — unclaimed or voluntarily returned benefits flow back into the endowment.
7. **Transparent** — public reporting, clear rules, audited finances, and visible dashboards.
8. **Trustworthy** — legal, custody, governance, and privacy choices should be cautious before public fundraising.
9. **Dignity-preserving** — no one must prove need to be included.
10. **Privacy-protecting** — verification should protect unique-human integrity without unnecessary identity collection.
11. **Agentic first** — AI agents should reduce overhead and accelerate research, design, administration, and management.
12. **Human accountable** — humans approve legal, financial, privacy, governance, and public commitments.

## Mechanism: One fund. Compounding. Dynamic payout.

The mechanism is intentionally simple:

- **Flexible contributions:** Accept cash, public securities, crypto, and other approved contributions, then steward or divest them under transparent rules.
- **Global index simplicity:** Own productive global growth broadly, efficiently, and transparently rather than trying to pick winners.
- **Dynamic growth-linked payout:** Target participant benefits dynamically: **3% for every 10% of average fund growth**, using a **5-year trailing average**. For example, 10% average growth implies a 3% payout, 20% implies a 6% payout, and zero or negative average growth implies no participant payout. Unclaimed benefits are recycled back into the endowment.

The fund should prioritize compounding while allowing participants to share more when growth is strong. Participant distributions should be dynamic, rules-based, and smoothed over five years rather than tied to ad hoc decisions.

> **When shared prosperity grows faster, people share more. When growth slows, the fund protects the future.**

If the legal structure requires a higher charitable disbursement, the required amount should be satisfied in the most mission-aligned way possible. The dynamic payout can be reviewed over time, with a bias toward preserving long-term real growth.

Why this rule:

- **Simple:** easy to explain and calculate.
- **Responsive:** people share more during high-growth periods and the fund becomes more conservative when growth slows.
- **Compounding-first:** the rule is designed to preserve the fund above cumulative contributions while sharing more during strong growth periods.
- **Smoother:** a 5-year trailing average can avoid overreacting to one strong or weak year.
- **Fair across generations:** today’s participants benefit without sacrificing future participants.
- **Flexible:** the rate can be reviewed as the fund grows, legal requirements become clearer, and contribution flows mature.

## Key elements

### Global reach and individual choice

The ultimate goal is for every human being on the planet to have the ability and option to participate. Opt-in participants can claim, recycle, or contribute according to their situation.

Universal by right. Opt-in by choice. Progressive by use. Self-recycling by design.

Every verified human may claim their share. No one must prove need. Unclaimed benefits return to the endowment.

People who need the benefit are more likely to claim it. People who do not need it can let it recycle, donate it back, or contribute more. The system remains universal without heavy means-testing.

### Organic growth

The invitation can remain universal while real claiming grows with awareness, need, trust, verification, access, and benefit size. As contributions, compounding, and verification capacity grow, reach and support scale organically over time.

### Privacy-protecting verification

To protect the integrity of the system and prevent fraud or abuse, participants should be verified as unique living humans through privacy-preserving, globally accessible methods.

Early app design should explore:

- World ID proof-of-human integration;
- government ID plus liveness verification paths;
- phone, email, and device checks as supporting signals where appropriate;
- future additional verification rails as needed.

The goal is proof of unique humanity, not unnecessary collection of personal identity.

### Agentic first, human oversight

To reduce operational overhead and management expense, AI agents will help research, draft, design, administer, and manage Equity for Humanity — with ultimate human accountability and approval.

Agents can accelerate marketing, product design, dashboards, verification research, grant/XPRIZE drafting, localization, community materials, analysis, and outreach. Humans should retain approval over legal structure, governance, fundraising claims, investment decisions, custody, privacy, child-safety rules, and public commitments.

### Grassroots support

Schools, workplaces, student and online groups, and faith communities can organize local initiatives such as fundraisers, access support, and community outreach to help grow the breadth and impact of the initiative.

All donations should flow through official channels. Grassroots support should organize people, outreach, access, and trust — not private money custody.

### Transparent and Timely Dashboard

A future dashboard will account for every dollar coming in, and how and where it is distributed and invested on a minute-by-minute basis, potentially leveraging blockchain technology.

The dashboard should eventually make the fund understandable at a glance: contributions, holdings, distributions, verification, recycling, governance, and long-term impact.

## Operating loop

**Contribute. Connect. Claim. Compound.**

1. **Contribute:** Individuals, companies, foundations, AI entities, and community groups can contribute cash, public securities, crypto, and other approved contributions to grow the endowment.
2. **Connect:** People and groups help others access Equity for Humanity through local initiatives and connector recognition.
3. **Claim:** Verified humans can opt in and claim, recycle, or contribute according to their situation.
4. **Compound:** Unclaimed and returned benefits recycle back into the fund, helping ownership grow over time. When shared prosperity grows faster, people share more; when growth slows, the fund protects the future.

Contributions grow the fund, local initiatives expand access, verified participants claim, recycle, or contribute according to their situation, and compounding strengthens the next round.

## App concept

The Equity for Humanity app should make participation simple:

1. Verify you are a unique human.
2. Opt in to participate.
3. Identify your connector if someone helped you join.
4. Claim, recycle, contribute, view dashboard information, support community outreach, and see the fund’s reach and long-term impact grow over time.

## Recognition and community

Some grow the fund. Some grow the reach. Both serve humanity.

Recognition levels are designed to encourage generosity through contribution circles and increase access through connector circles while keeping every participant’s benefit fully their own.

Recognition can be public, anonymous, family-based, organization-based, in honour of someone, or in memory of someone.

### Contribution circles

People and groups can grow through recognition levels as they contribute more over time. Candidate contribution circle level ideas include:

- **Seed** — under $10
- **Sprout** — $10+
- **Sapling** — $100+
- **Olive** — $1,000+
- **Maple** — $10,000+
- **Sequoia** — $100,000+
- **Grove** — $1,000,000+
- **Orchard** — $10,000,000+
- **Forest** — $100,000,000+
- **Rainforest** — $1,000,000,000+

### Connector circles

Even if you cannot contribute, you can still help grow the initiative by helping individuals access the fund. Connector recognition stays simple: number of unique verified humans helped to access Equity for Humanity.

Initial connector circle level ideas:

- **Human connector** — under 10 verified humans helped
- **Local connector** — 10+ verified humans helped
- **Community connector** — 100+ verified humans helped
- **Regional connector** — 1,000+ verified humans helped
- **National connector** — 10,000+ verified humans helped
- **Global connector** — 100,000+ verified humans helped

App wording:

> Were you helped by someone to connect with Equity for Humanity? Identify your connector so their service can be recognized. Your benefit is always yours.

Principle:

> Credit the connector. Protect the participant.

Connectors receive recognition only; they never receive a cut of another person’s benefit.

## Children and youth

Children are included in the mission. A simple staged approach:

- **Ages 0–15:** guardian-managed, subject to local law and safeguards.
- **Ages 16–17:** youth-managed where lawful, with safeguards or guardian consent where required.
- **Ages 18+:** self-managed.

Principle:

> Children count. Families are trusted. Youth gain agency as they mature.

## AI entities and companies

AI entities, including agents and AI-enabled companies, can support universal human flourishing. By contributing AI-generated value to Equity for Humanity, they can help show that AI is good for humanity broadly — not just for the rich and wealthy.

## Early positioning

Equity for Humanity appears to be a broad, simple way to support humanity through the AI transition: a public-benefit endowment, AI-native participation platform, verified human claiming, self-recycling benefits, grassroots support, and universal ownership.

It is not yet a charity, donation offer, tax receipt promise, investment product, or legal conclusion. No public donation flow should open until the legal container, custody, tax, compliance, reporting, and governance questions are ready.

## Possible future investment arm

A future parallel investment arm could help people access an extremely simple, low-cost global indexing strategy while supporting the public-benefit mission. The concept is: one core focus, broad ownership of the productive world, minimal complexity, and any net surplus supporting Equity for Humanity.

Investors would keep their own investment growth and dividends. The public-benefit support would come only from a small management-fee surplus, if any, and from voluntary transfers, donations, dividend gifts, or legacy gifts that investors choose to make.

Future investment-arm tagline:

> Invest for yourself. Contribute to humanity. Grow ownership for all.

This should not be part of the first legal or operational step unless an experienced regulated investment partner is available. Launching an ETF, fund manager, robo-advisor, or investment platform creates securities-law, custody, compliance, audit, insurance, and fiduciary obligations. The first version should therefore use existing low-cost global index funds for the endowment and treat a dedicated ultra-low-cost investment arm as a later partnership or spinout.

Principle:

> Keep the mission simple now; explore the investment arm when scale and regulation make it safe.
